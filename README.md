# Rise-1-Million-is-a-global-research-initiative
About the Project
This project addresses the critical challenge of Human Energy Sustainability™ in leadership roles. Working with the global research initiative Rise 1 Million, our team analyzed how organizations measure, predict, and prevent leadership fatigue and performance decline. The primary objective was to bridge the "measurement gap" where traditional KPIs (revenue, productivity) are tracked, but the human energy required to sustain them is ignored.

My Role: Lead Data Analyst & Dashboard Designer In this collaborative capstone project, my primary contributions included:
Statistical Modeling: Performing OLS regression analysis to identify relationships between professional experience and leadership endurance.
Data Processing: Cleaning and categorizing survey data from 117 cross-industry participants, including the handling of high-frequency "Not Tracked" responses as meaningful analytical signals.Dashboard Architecture: Designing the Executive Burnout Dashboard and defining the mathematical formulas for key metrics like the Sustainable Energy Ratio (SER).Technical Documentation: Ensuring all analytical workflows complied with PIPEDA and ethical data handling standards.

Project Highlights
Identification of Measurement Gaps: Discovered that 86.9% of organizations do not track human energy or burnout risk, revealing a massive blind spot in corporate risk management.
Fatigue Impact Correlation: Proved a statistically significant relationship ($p < 0.001$) showing that higher leadership experience slightly reduces the impact of fatigue on strategic errors.
Custom Analytical Framework: Developed the Executive Burnout Dashboard, which provides four proprietary metrics to help organizations move from "enduring" to "performing".
Insignificant Predictors: Successfully demonstrated through OLS modeling that years of experience have almost zero correlation with current energy levels, suggesting external organizational factors play a much larger role than personal tenure.
